What we found

Loneliness is a silent health crisis

After visiting Hakametsä Sports Centre and spending time with elderly residents in Tampere, our team identified a pattern that doesn't show up on x-rays — but causes real, measurable physiological harm.

🏠
Social isolation
Many elderly residents in Tampere live alone. As friends and family members pass away, social circles shrink dramatically — leaving few regular human interactions each week.
"They would light up when someone just sat down and asked how they were doing."
💭
Unspoken depression
Grief from losing spouses, siblings, and friends accumulates silently. Many elderly people don't want to be a burden — so they hold it in, making it hard for professionals to recognise and help.
"They don't want to share much — they feel they shouldn't complain, even when they're struggling."
🩺
Invisible physiological signals
Chronic loneliness elevates cortisol, disrupts heart rate variability, and raises skin conductance baselines. These stress responses often go undetected until they become serious clinical conditions.
"We needed an objective way to see what their bodies were telling us — not just their words."
Our solution

A community social event with purpose

Not a clinical study. Not a hospital visit. A warm, informal gathering where elderly residents come for free coffee, fresh pulla, good conversation — and where we quietly listen to what their bodies have to say.

The WellSense Band — how it works

A lightweight wrist-worn device designed specifically for comfortable elderly use. Four sensors record physiological signals continuously and transmit encrypted data wirelessly to the research backend every 30 seconds.

💚
PPG Sensor (Photoplethysmography)
Green & red LEDs measure heart rate and HRV — beat-to-beat intervals reveal cardiovascular stress responses and engagement levels.
HR · HRV · SpO₂
⚡
EDA Sensor (Electrodermal Activity)
Dry electrodes on the inner wrist measure skin conductance. Detects sympathetic nervous system arousal — the body's hidden stress indicator.
Skin conductance · Stress index
🌡
Skin Temperature Sensor
Infrared thermistor captures peripheral temperature changes. Drops correlate with stress-induced vasoconstriction — cold hands under anxiety.
°C · Thermoregulation
📐
3-Axis Accelerometer
Tracks wrist movement and activity levels throughout the event. Used to assess physical engagement and separate rest phases from active social interaction.
Movement · Activity · Engagement
Registration flow — event day
🪪
Arrive & check in
Participant arrives at Hakametsä. Staff assigns session ID using only initials and age group — no full name required. Privacy first.
📲
Pair the band
Staff tablet auto-scans nearby WellSense devices via BLE. Band assigned to participant profile in one tap — under 20 seconds.
✅
Consent & baseline
Participant gives verbal consent confirmed digitally. 2-minute seated baseline recorded — calibrates sensors to the individual.
📡
Live monitoring
Data streams via BLE to the hub tablet then to the cloud every 30 seconds. The dashboard updates live for medical staff on-site.
Research monitoring tool

Live well-being dashboard

Medical staff and researchers monitor all participants in real time during the social event. Stress flags are raised automatically so the on-site doctor can check in discreetly — no disruption to the event.

Expected outcomes

What we hope to prove

Beyond the event itself, the physiological data collected provides objective evidence that structured social interaction measurably improves elderly well-being — findings that can shape city policy in Tampere and beyond.

Project timeline
Phase 1 — Complete
Community networking at Hakametsä
Team visited Hakametsä Sports Centre, spoke with elderly residents and medical professionals. Identified loneliness and unspoken depression as the primary challenges to address.
Phase 2 — In progress
Partnership formation & event planning
Collaborations established with City of Tampere, Pirha, Hakametsä, and NYSSE. Event format confirmed: informal social gathering with free coffee, pulla, and on-site medical staff.
Phase 3 — Engineering
Wearable device & dashboard development
Design and prototype of the WellSense Band, staff registration app, cloud data pipeline, and real-time monitoring dashboard. Ethics review and GDPR compliance for data collection.
Phase 4 — Upcoming
Social event & live data collection
The event is held at Hakametsä. Participants arrive, register voluntarily, and enjoy the gathering while biosignals are collected. Medical staff are present throughout. NYSSE provides free transport.
Phase 5 — Research output
Data analysis & publication
Physiological data analysed to quantify the effect of structured social interaction on elderly well-being. Findings shared with Tampere University and city partners to inform future programmes.
